Project background

Decode anxiety with data, design certainty with intuition

Users in their 20s to 40s who have narrowed down a car model hesitate before purchase because the unfamiliar financial system of residual-value installment creates vague anxiety.

  • Opaque information: lack of clear interest calculations and future value details
  • Risk fear: worry about additional costs that may occur in 3–5 years
  • No comparison standard: no objective way to confirm whether this choice fits their income level
Scope of work & My role

Deliverables and my contribution

  • Received detailed user data, personas, behavioral analysis, and real customer cases directly from the client.
  • Focused on IA, UI, visual tone & manner, and landing page information architecture and visualization.
  • Built a design system for colors, layouts, typography, and buttons aligned with brand and legal guidelines.
  • Enhanced visual decision-making elements such as product comparison tables, monthly payment simulations, and life-event-based plans.
  • Designed CTA, flow, FAQ, and actual purchase/contract scenarios with the user journey in mind.

Problem Statement

Why Users Abandoned the Journey

Users had access to sufficient data — yet they consistently left without converting. The challenge was not a lack of information, but a failure to make that information feel relevant and trustworthy.

Core Challenge: Users abandoned the purchase journey despite sufficient data availability.

1Complex interest calculations and insufficient future value information
2Opaque financial details creating uncertainty and distrust
3Vague anxiety about costs emerging 3–5 years later
4Absence of objective benchmarks for income-level appropriateness
5Unfamiliarity with "残価設定型割賦" (residual value credit) financing system

Solution

02
